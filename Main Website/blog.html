Nagpur University Postponed Papers

University
March 26, 2019

In the wake of coronavirus outbreak, many higher educational institutes have either directed students to vacate campus or have cancelled events. The Rashtrasant Tukadoji Maharaj Nagpur University on Monday postponed all its summer exams. The Registrar of the varsity Niraj Khaty said a total of 187 examinations which were supposed to start from March 19 have been postponed. “This will affect around 90000 students,” he said.

7 days Skill Development Workshop

Updates in IT
March 26, 2020

The Department of Information Technology, had organized an industry collaborated 50hrs (7 days) skill development workshop on “Augmented Reality and Virtual Reality using Vuforia & Unity”, from 29th February, 2020 to 15th March, 2020. The workshop’s facilitators were Mr. Rahul Chute, LDT Program Director & Mr. Nikhil Wadnerkar, Dnnovate Technologies, Bangalore. Mr. Rahul Chute gave them a detailed overview about Augmented Reality Applications. On the first day of the workshop students were taught about the unity and vuforia installation. During the hands on session, students created various 3D objects in unity game engine using Blender, this was a thrilling experience for them. 67 students of 2nd, 3rd & 4th year participated in this workshop. Students also developed AR applications like projection of college video & audio recitation of college vision & mission. They were more than content with the deliverables and enjoyed the workshop to the fullest. This workshop was co-ordinated by faculty members Prof. Shabana Pathan & Prof. Manali Patki.

Expert lecture on BS6 and EATS familiarization

Updates in Mechanical
March 16, 2020

An expert lecture on BS6 and EATS (Exhaust After Treatment Systems) familiarization was delivered by Mr. Varun Raval, alumnus (2009 batch) ETC Department. He is a Strategist Manager at VE Commercial Vehicles Ltd. (A Volvo Group and Eicher Motors JV). Students of the 6th semester from Mechanical Engineering and Electronics Telecommunication Department benefitted through this session. Mr. Raval shared his journey of engineering education and career in the automobile industry after completing his engineering in E&TC. He further narrated his own experiences of the interview process and ways to get successful in any interview after engineering education. He also gave insights on current trends in the industry and key areas of employment in the coming years. The alumnus introduced students to new and advanced perspectives of the automobile autonomy and spoke about Euro and Bharat Stage Emission Norms. By sharing the pollution scenario from the automobile industry, he justified the need of switching from BS-4 to BS-6 directly to reduce pollution emitted from vehicles in order to target high life expectancy of human beings. For reducing this pollution and harmful gases emitted from engine exhaust the comparison between Petrol and Diesel engine exhaust gases were discussed.
